Learning Noise-Invariant Representations for Unified Speech Enhancement and Text-to-Speech

Abstract

Zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) systems exhibit limited noise-robustness. Existing approaches address this through extra processing or complex decoupling, leading to higher computation cost and artifacts. In this paper, we propose a unified joint speech enhancement (SE) and TTS model based on conditional flow matching. Our method enables single-step SE while jointly learning noise-invariant linguistic and speaker representations for TTS. To further enforce semantic consistency, we introduce a Paired Align-Uniform Loss, which pulls together sub-positive pairs generated from the same noisy prompt while maintaining separation across different prompts. In addition, Environment Fréchet Coefficient is proposed as an objective metric to evaluate background retention quality, complementing existing subjective metrics. Experimental results show that the proposed model can generate high-quality audio under diverse noisy conditions in a controllable manner.
